# PDF Design Audit - Coaching Reports

Date reviewed: 2026-05-07
Scope: Daily manager summary, daily rep summaries, optional call-scorecard packet, and combined packet generated by `scripts/reports/generate_pdfs.py`.

## Executive Summary

The V1 PDF reports were content-rich but visually flat: heavy table framing, weak score semantics, inconsistent hierarchy, and operational filtering metadata on the manager-facing surface. The redesign moves the PDFs toward a coaching product interface: consistent report headers, shared typography, semantic score bands, cleaner cards, clearer executive summaries, and manager/rep layouts that support fast scanning.

Operational filtering details are now retained in `manifest.json` only. They are not shown in manager, rep, call-scorecard, or combined PDFs.

## Cohesion Diagnosis

The most important cohesion improvement is that score meaning is now consistent everywhere:

- Green means `Strong`.
- Gold means `Watch`.
- Red means `Priority`.
- Every color-coded score also includes a text label, so meaning is not color-only.

The daily report cadence is also reflected visually:

- Manager PDFs focus on team pattern, rep priority, and detail appendix.
- Rep PDFs focus on one highest-leverage improvement and compliance watchouts.
- Individual call scorecards are opt-in review artifacts, not part of default daily delivery.

## Top Recommendations

1. Keep individual call cards out of default daily PDFs. Use Slack/UI for individual call review.
2. Keep manager reports focused on included sales calls only. Do not surface excluded-call metadata in the report body.
3. Use score bands consistently across future weekly, monthly, and quarterly PDFs.
4. If the reporting layer moves to a frontend, consider an HTML-to-PDF renderer for richer charts, fixed card heights, and better responsive visual testing.

## Acceptance Criteria

- No manager-facing PDF includes `Excluded from this report`, `Filtered for sales coaching`, or removed-rep details.
- No PDF exposes raw `acti_` call IDs in visible report copy.
- Manager, rep, scorecard, and combined PDFs share the same header, font, metric, score, card, and footer primitives.
- Score state is never color-only; each score includes `Strong`, `Watch`, or `Priority`.
- Rep detail appendix pages do not orphan rep headers at the bottom of a page.
- Optional call-scorecard packets have a cover/summary page before call-level detail starts.
